Why are the Parenting Plan Form and Time-Sharing Plans Important?

The parenting plan form is a legal binding agreement that lays out most decisions relating to the children and the responsibilities of the parents. It is extremely important to create a sustainable plan in the best interest of the child, as after the plan is approved/created by a judge, a breach in the plan is considered contempt of court.

The general time-sharing schedule determines the time-sharing of the children. This website provides over 100 options on how the time with the children can be laid out. Quality time with the children is important to keep in mind when looking at the schedules instead of the overall time with the child.

These documents have lasting impacts on the children and your life moving forward. Much care should be taken to propose a suitable plan focused on the best interest of the children.

Step 1: Physical Custody Type

Parents choose which physical custody type they are seeking.

Step 2: Age Category of Child(ren)

These plans are divided into four key age categories, with additional categories including a multiple age category and unique work situations. The plans are organized into age categories with the best interests of the child's development in mind.

Step 3: General Time-Sharing Schedule

Parents select a general time-sharing schedule, which includes a pdf form that can be attached to the model parenting plan form. Parents can select a plan together or independently.

Step 4: Combine Model Parenting Plan Form and General Time-Sharing Schedule

Parents include their selected time-sharing schedule in the parenting plan form.

Step 5: Submit Parenting Plan and Schedule to Judge
